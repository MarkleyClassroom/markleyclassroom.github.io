'Wait is over' message appears as Woodruff gets new owner

4:32 PM, Nov 7, 2012   |

Written by

  • Filed Under

  • Ozarks News

An upbeat message has appeared spray-painted on one of downtown Springfield’s iconic buildings — just days after records show the vacant building changed ownership.

Crude black letters spray-painted beneath the windows on the upper two floors of the Woodruff building appear to spell out “Memo — The wait is over!” Online documents from the Greene County Recorder’s Office, meanwhile, reveal that the building got a new owner last week, raising the question of whether the message and the transfer are related.

It’s not clear when the message first appeared; a News-Leader photographer first spotted it this morning.

Previous owners Brett and Bart Loethen, doing business as Downtown Springfield Property LLC, filed paperwork Oct. 31 granting a deed to the property to Woodruff Historical Building LLC. The Secretary of State’s records show the new Woodruff corporation was organized in May by developer Matt Miller, who also was involved in the purchase of the nearby McDaniel building from the Loethens in late June.

Miller, who has been involved with the renovation of a number of large downtown properties, currently is working with his wife, Stacy, on a plan to convert the Landmark Building — just north of the Woodruff — into 68 affordable housing units accessible to tenants with disabilities.

Neither of the Millers immediately responded to a call requesting comment on both the transfer of ownership or the message.

Rusty Worley, executive director of the Urban Districts Alliance, said that Miller plans to convert much of the buildings into residential lofts.

“They’re looking for the building to be residential, with commercial space on the first floor,” he said.

Worley wasn’t aware whether the message had to do with the sale, but said that staff members were working to remove other graffiti that had appeared downtown recently.

The 10-story Woodruff building was built in 1909 and saw its last tenants move out a century later. It stands at the corner of Jefferson Avenue and St. Louis Street, east of Park Central Square, and is one of the “Big Four” downtown, along with the McDaniel, Landmark and Heer’s buildings.

"We’re excited about a new chapter in that storied building’s history,” Worley said.
